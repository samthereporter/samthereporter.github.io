The Empty Desk Epidemic: Tracing Chronic Absenteeism in Nebraska

Before the pandemic, chronic absenteeism in Nebraska hovered around 10%. Today, classrooms have returned to normal, but student attendance hasn't.

A Statewide Challenge

It is a common misconception that chronic absenteeism is primarily an urban issue. In Nebraska, the data tells a different story.

Urban counties like Lancaster and Douglas show similar rates to rural neighbors. The highest rates? Thurston County (48%) and McPherson County (46%).

The Middle School Cliff

Attendance habits shift dramatically as students grow older. A significant 5% spike occurs during the transition to middle school.

By 12th grade, the chronic absenteeism rate peaks at 36.98%.

Why Are They Missing?

Typically-attending students miss school for illness. Chronic absentees miss school for different reasons.

Skipping school is 5x higher for chronic absentees, and "Unknown absences" account for 40.7% of their missed time.

The Academic Toll

By the 11th grade, the achievement gap is glaring. Proficiency in Math, Science, and English drops significantly.

"Only 20.1% of chronically absent 11th graders are proficient in math."

Graduation & Beyond

The odds of graduating on-time were six times greater for students who attended regularly.

For chronic absentees, postsecondary enrollment drops to just 48.7%.

Reversing the Trend

The Nebraska Department of Education aims to cut chronic absenteeism in half by investing in early interventions and removing access barriers.
